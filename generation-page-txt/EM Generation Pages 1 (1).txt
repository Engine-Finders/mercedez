SECTION 3 — OVERVIEW — <Overview>
H2: BMW 2 Series Active Tourer F45 — Overview
The F45 is BMW's first front‑wheel‑drive production car — a five‑door, two‑row compact MPV launched in August 2014. It marked a significant shift for BMW, moving away from rear‑wheel drive to the UKL2 platform shared with the Mini Countryman, in order to maximise interior space and practicality. Designed to compete directly with the Mercedes‑Benz B‑Class, the Active Tourer offered a spacious cabin, sliding rear seats, and a premium feel. Under the bonnet, the F45 featured BMW's latest modular engine family — the B37, B38, B47 and B48 — across a range of petrol, diesel and plug‑in hybrid variants. A 2018 facelift brought revised styling and updated engine outputs. Production ran until 2021, when the second‑generation U06 replaced it.
Key Facts: Platform: BMW UKL2 (front‑wheel drive) • Predecessor: None (first‑generation Active Tourer) • Successor: BMW U06 • Sibling body styles in this generation: F46 (Gran Tourer, three‑row MPV).
