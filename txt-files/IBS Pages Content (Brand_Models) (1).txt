SECTION 13: DISCOVERY FAQ — <FAQAccordion> (8 questions)
H2: Discovery Frequently Asked Questions
1. How much does a Discovery engine replacement cost in the UK?
 A reconditioned Discovery engine typically costs £2,500–£9,500 supply only, depending on generation and engine [EM-QUOTE]. Tdi-era units sit at £2,500–£4,500; the 3.0 diesels at £6,500–£9,500. Fitting adds £650–£1,900. Use our Discovery calculator for a personalised figure.
2. Which Discovery engine is the most reliable?
 Historically, the 300Tdi — mechanically injected and endlessly rebuildable. In the modern range, the AJ20D6 D300 (2021+) is proving the most dependable, followed by post-2019 Ingenium four-cylinders. (Note: the 4.4 SDV8 often cited as JLR's most reliable engine was never fitted to a Discovery.)
3. Which Discovery engine should I avoid?
 The 2.7 TDV6 (276DT) and 3.0 TDV6/SDV6 (306DT) carry the highest catastrophic-failure risk — oil pump failure and crankshaft bearing failure respectively, both capable of leaving nothing to rebuild. Neither is a reason to walk away from a documented car; both are reasons to walk away from an undocumented one.
4. How do I know if my Discovery engine needs replacing?
 Warning signs by engine: cold-start knock (306DT), sudden oil-pressure loss (276DT), cold-start chain rattle (pre-2019 204DTD), blue smoke or excessive oil consumption (any). Use our diagnostic calculator for a personalised assessment.
5. Is it worth replacing a Discovery 4 engine?
 It depends on the car's value. A Discovery 4 worth £10,000–£18,000 justifies a £6,500–£9,500 reconditioned SDV6; a £6,000 car sits at our 60% scrap threshold [EM-QUOTE]. Use the Ownership Decision Tool for a personalised verdict.
6. Which Discovery generation should I buy?
 Discovery 5 (2021+) for the best all-round profile on the AJ20D6. Discovery 4 SDV6 for value — only with proven oil-interval history. Avoid any Discovery 3 or early 4 on the 2.7 TDV6 without full service history.
7. How do I find my Discovery engine code?
 Stamped on the engine block (typically the left side) and listed on the VIN plate and vehicle documentation — or tell us your variant (TDV6, SDV6, D300 etc.) and we'll identify it.
8. Discovery 4 vs Discovery 5 — which is more reliable?
 Discovery 5, particularly from 2021 on the AJ20D6 six. Discovery 4 is cheaper to buy and more capable off-road out of the box, but the SDV6 carries real crankshaft-bearing risk at higher mileages. Service history matters more than the badge. Read the full head-to-head →

H2: Discovery Sport Frequently Asked Questions
1. How much does a Discovery Sport engine replacement cost in the UK?

2. Which Discovery Sport engine is the most reliable?
 The 204PT Ingenium petrol and post-2019 204DTD diesels. The 2019.5 facelift's revised chain hardware materially changed the diesel's reliability profile.
3. Which Discovery Sport engine should I avoid?
 No engine outright — but a pre-2019 204DTD diesel without a documented timing-chain inspection is the one purchase we consistently advise against. The engine is fine; the unknown is not.
4. How do I know if my Discovery Sport timing chain is worn?
 Cold-start rattle for the first few seconds is the classic early warning, sometimes with a chain-noise change under load. Caught early it's a repair; ignored, it's a £3,500–£5,800 engine [EM-QUOTE]. Use our diagnostic calculator for a personalised assessment.
5. Is it worth replacing a Discovery Sport engine?
 Usually yes — £3,500–£5,800 against typical £10,000+ values clears our 60% scrap-threshold rule. The honest exception is the cheapest 2015–2016 cars, where a worst-case quote brushes the threshold. Use the Ownership Decision Tool for your car.
6. Which year Discovery Sport should I buy?
 2020 or later. You get the facelift architecture, mild-hybrid diesels with revised chain hardware, and the P300e option for short-run drivers. Among pre-facelift cars, insist on chain-inspection paperwork.
7. How do I find my Discovery Sport engine code?
 Listed on the VIN plate and in the vehicle documentation; the block stamping is accessible from below on the Ingenium units. Or tell us your variant (TD4, D180, P250 etc.) and we'll identify it.
